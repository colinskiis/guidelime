## Scope

This guideline provides updated recommendations for the diagnosis and treatment of sexually transmitted infections (STIs) in adolescents and adults.

## Key Recommendations

### Chlamydia trachomatis
**Treatment:**
- Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7 days (preferred)
- Azithromycin 1g single dose (alternative)
- Test of cure for pharyngeal infection and in pregnancy

### Neisseria gonorrhoeae
**Uncomplicated Urogenital/Rectal/Pharyngeal:**
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM single dose (weight <150kg)
- Ceftriaxone 1g IM if weight â‰¥150kg
- If chlamydia not ruled out: Add doxycycline

**Cephalosporin Allergy:**
- Gentamicin 240mg IM + azithromycin 2g PO

### Syphilis
**Primary/Secondary/Early Latent (<1 year):**
- Benzathine penicillin G 2.4 million units IM single dose

**Late Latent/Unknown Duration:**
- Benzathine penicillin G 2.4 million units IM weekly x 3 weeks

**Neurosyphilis:**
- Aqueous crystalline penicillin G IV 18-24 million units/day x 10-14 days

### Genital Herpes (HSV)
**First Episode:**
- Valacyclovir 1g BID x 7-10 days
- Acyclovir 400mg TID x 7-10 days

**Recurrent Episodes:**
- Valacyclovir 500mg BID x 3 days (episodic)
- Daily suppressive therapy for frequent recurrences

### Trichomoniasis
- Metronidazole 500mg BID x 7 days (women)
- Metronidazole 2g single dose (men)

### Partner Services
- Expedited partner therapy where legal
- HIV and other STI testing recommended
- Repeat testing at 3 months for gonorrhea/chlamydia
