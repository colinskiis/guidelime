# ESC Guidelines for the Management of Endocarditis

## Scope
Comprehensive guidelines for the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of infective endocarditis.

## Key Recommendations

### Diagnosis

#### Modified Duke Criteria (Updated)
**Major Criteria:**
1. Positive blood cultures
   - Typical organisms in ≥2 separate cultures
   - Persistently positive cultures
2. Imaging evidence
   - Vegetation, abscess, pseudoaneurysm, fistula
   - New valvular regurgitation
   - Prosthetic valve dehiscence

**Minor Criteria:**
- Predisposing condition
- Fever ≥38°C
- Vascular phenomena
- Immunologic phenomena
- Positive blood culture not meeting major criteria

#### Diagnostic Classification
| Category | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| Definite | 2 major, or 1 major + 3 minor, or 5 minor |
| Possible | 1 major + 1 minor, or 3 minor |
| Rejected | Alternative diagnosis, resolution with ≤4 days antibiotics |

### Imaging

#### Echocardiography (Strong)
- TTE as first-line
- TEE if TTE negative but high suspicion
- TEE for prosthetic valves
- TEE for assessment before surgery

#### Additional Imaging
| Modality | Indication |
|----------|------------|
| Cardiac CT | Perivalvular extension |
| 18F-FDG PET/CT | Prosthetic valve, device infection |
| Cerebral MRI | Suspected neurologic complications |

### Antimicrobial Therapy

#### Native Valve Endocarditis - Empiric
| Situation | Regimen |
|-----------|---------|
| Community-acquired | Ampicillin + (flu)cloxacillin + gentamicin |
| Healthcare-associated | Vancomycin + gentamicin |

#### Streptococcal Endocarditis (Penicillin-susceptible)
| Regimen | Duration |
|---------|----------|
| Penicillin G or ampicillin | 4 weeks |
| Ceftriaxone | 4 weeks |
| Penicillin + gentamicin | 2 weeks (uncomplicated) |

#### Staphylococcal Endocarditis
| Type | Regimen | Duration |
|------|---------|----------|
| MSSA native valve | (Flu)cloxacillin | 4-6 weeks |
| MRSA native valve | Vancomycin or daptomycin | 4-6 weeks |
| Prosthetic valve | Vancomycin + rifampin + gentamicin | ≥6 weeks |

#### Enterococcal Endocarditis
| Susceptibility | Regimen | Duration |
|----------------|---------|----------|
| Ampicillin-susceptible | Ampicillin + ceftriaxone | 6 weeks |
| High-level gentamicin resistance | Ampicillin + ceftriaxone | 6 weeks |
| VRE | Daptomycin-based | 6 weeks |

### Surgical Indications

#### Urgent Surgery (Within 24-48 Hours)
- Severe heart failure due to valve dysfunction
- Fungal or resistant organism endocarditis
- Uncontrolled infection (abscess, enlarging vegetation)

#### Early Surgery (During Hospitalization)
- Large vegetation (>10 mm) with embolic event or high risk
- Severe valve regurgitation without heart failure
- Perivalvular extension

#### Surgery Considerations
- Multidisciplinary team decision
- Timing based on individual risk assessment
- Neurologic status evaluation before surgery

### Complications

#### Neurologic
- Ischemic stroke
- Hemorrhagic stroke
- Mycotic aneurysm
- Meningitis
- Brain abscess

#### Cardiac
- Heart failure
- Abscess formation
- Fistula
- Conduction abnormalities

#### Systemic
- Septic emboli
- Splenic abscess
- Renal failure
- Septic shock

### Device-Related Endocarditis

#### Pacemaker/ICD Infection
- Complete device removal recommended
- Lead extraction
- Prolonged antibiotics (4-6 weeks)
- Reimplantation after blood cultures negative

### Prophylaxis

#### High-Risk Patients
- Prosthetic valves
- Previous endocarditis
- Congenital heart disease (specific types)
- Cardiac transplant with valvulopathy

#### Dental Procedures (High-Risk Patients)
| Drug | Dose |
|------|------|
| Amoxicillin | 2g PO 30-60 min before |
| Penicillin allergy: Clindamycin | 600 mg PO |

#### Procedures NOT Requiring Prophylaxis
- Gastrointestinal procedures
- Genitourinary procedures
- Dermatologic procedures
- Respiratory procedures (most)

### Follow-Up
- Clinical assessment after treatment
- Echocardiography at end of therapy
- Regular surveillance for prosthetic valves
- Dental health maintenance

### Multidisciplinary Approach
- Endocarditis team recommended
- Cardiology, cardiac surgery, infectious disease
- Early surgical consultation
