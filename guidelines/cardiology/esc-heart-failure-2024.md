## Scope
2024 focused update to the ESC guidelines on the diagnosis and treatment of chronic heart failure.

## Key Recommendations

### Classification
- **HFrEF**: LVEF ≤40%.
- **HFmrEF**: LVEF 41-49%.
- **HFpEF**: LVEF ≥50%.

### Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT) for HFrEF

#### Four Pillars (All Should Be Initiated)
1. **ACE Inhibitor (or ARB) / ARNI**: Sacubitril-valsartan (ARNI) is preferred over ACEi/ARB for most patients.
2. **Beta-Blocker**: Carvedilol, bisoprolol, metoprolol succinate, or nebivolol.
3. **Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist (MRA)**: Spironolactone or eplerenone.
4. **SGLT2 Inhibitor**: Dapagliflozin or empagliflozin. Now a class I recommendation for HFrEF regardless of diabetes status.

- Initiate all four classes early, ideally before hospital discharge following HF hospitalization.
- Titrate to target doses as tolerated.

### HFmrEF and HFpEF
- **SGLT2 Inhibitors**: Class I recommendation across the HF spectrum (HFrEF, HFmrEF, HFpEF).
- Diuretics for congestion management.
- Treat underlying comorbidities.

### Device Therapy
- **ICD**: For primary prevention in HFrEF (LVEF ≤35%) despite ≥3 months of GDMT.
- **CRT**: For HFrEF with LBBB and QRS ≥150 ms.

### Iron Deficiency
- IV iron (ferric carboxymaltose or ferric derisomaltose) is recommended for symptomatic HF patients with iron deficiency (ferritin <100 or ferritin 100-299 with TSAT <20%).

### Diuretics
- Loop diuretics for congestion symptom relief; titrate to euvolemia.
