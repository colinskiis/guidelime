# EAU Guidelines on Urological Infections

## Scope
Evidence-based recommendations for diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of urinary tract infections and male accessory gland infections in adults.

## Key Recommendations

### Classification of UTI

#### By Location
- **Lower UTI**: Cystitis, urethritis
- **Upper UTI**: Pyelonephritis
- **Male genital infections**: Prostatitis, epididymitis, orchitis

#### By Complicating Factors
- **Uncomplicated**: Healthy, non-pregnant women with normal urinary tract
- **Complicated**: Structural/functional abnormality, catheter, pregnancy, male sex, immunocompromise

### Acute Uncomplicated Cystitis (Women)

#### Diagnosis
- Clinical symptoms (dysuria, frequency, urgency) usually sufficient
- Urine culture not routinely required for uncomplicated cases
- Consider culture if recurrent or treatment failure

#### First-Line Treatment
| Antibiotic | Dose | Duration |
|------------|------|----------|
| Fosfomycin | 3g single dose | 1 day |
| Nitrofurantoin | 100mg BID | 5 days |
| Pivmecillinam | 400mg BID | 3-5 days |

#### Alternatives (Not First-Line)
- TMP-SMX (if local resistance <20%)
- Fluoroquinolones (reserve for other indications)

### Acute Uncomplicated Pyelonephritis

#### Diagnosis
- Flank pain, fever, CVA tenderness
- Urine culture and susceptibility testing required
- Consider imaging if no improvement in 48-72 hours

#### Outpatient Treatment (Mild-Moderate)
| Antibiotic | Duration |
|------------|----------|
| Fluoroquinolone | 5-7 days |
| TMP-SMX | 14 days |
| Oral beta-lactam | 10-14 days |

#### Inpatient Treatment (Severe)
- IV fluoroquinolone, aminoglycoside, or extended-spectrum cephalosporin
- Switch to oral when clinically improved
- Total duration: 7-14 days

### Complicated UTI

#### Principles
- Identify and address complicating factor
- Obtain urine culture before treatment
- Longer treatment duration (7-14 days typically)

#### Catheter-Associated UTI
- Remove or replace catheter if possible
- Treat only if symptomatic (not asymptomatic bacteriuria)
- Duration: 7 days (14 days if delayed response)

### Recurrent UTI (≥3/year or ≥2 in 6 months)

#### Behavioral Measures
- Increase fluid intake
- Post-coital voiding
- Avoid spermicides

#### Prophylaxis Options
- **Continuous**: Low-dose antibiotics (nitrofurantoin, TMP-SMX)
- **Post-coital**: Single dose after intercourse
- **Non-antibiotic**: Cranberry products, D-mannose, vaginal estrogen (postmenopausal)

### Acute Bacterial Prostatitis

#### Diagnosis
- Fever, perineal/pelvic pain, voiding symptoms
- Tender prostate on exam (avoid vigorous massage)
- Urine culture

#### Treatment
- Fluoroquinolone or TMP-SMX preferred (good prostate penetration)
- Duration: **2-4 weeks**
- Consider drainage if abscess present

### Chronic Bacterial Prostatitis

#### Diagnosis
- Recurrent UTI with same organism
- 4-glass or 2-glass test

#### Treatment
- Fluoroquinolone for **4-6 weeks**
- TMP-SMX alternative

### Epididymitis/Orchitis

#### Sexually Transmitted
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM single dose + doxycycline 100mg BID x 10 days

#### Enteric Organisms (Older men, recent instrumentation)
- Fluoroquinolone x 10-14 days

### Asymptomatic Bacteriuria

#### DO NOT Treat (in most cases)
- Elderly
- Diabetics
- Catheterized patients
- Spinal cord injury

#### DO Treat
- Pregnant women
- Before urological procedures with mucosal bleeding
