## Scope
2024 AUA guideline update on the medical management and prevention of recurrent kidney stones.

## Key Recommendations

### Evaluation
- **First Stone Episode**: Serum chemistry (calcium, uric acid, creatinine), urinalysis, stone analysis when available.
- **Recurrent Stone Formers or High-Risk Patients**: 24-hour urine collection(s) to identify metabolic abnormalities (hypercalciuria, hyperoxaluria, hypocitraturia, hyperuricosuria, low urine volume, high urine pH, cystinuria).

### General Prevention (All Stone Formers)
- **Fluid Intake**: Drink enough to produce â‰¥2.5 L urine/day. Water is preferred.
- **Dietary Modifications**:
  - Limit sodium (<2300 mg/day).
  - Moderate animal protein intake.
  - Maintain adequate dietary calcium (1000-1200 mg/day from food, not supplements).
  - Limit oxalate-rich foods if hyperoxaluria (spinach, nuts, chocolate).
  - Increase fruits and vegetables (citrate sources).

### Pharmacologic Therapy (Based on 24-Hour Urine Abnormalities)

#### Hypercalciuria
- **Thiazide Diuretics (Chlorthalidone, Indapamide, Hydrochlorothiazide)**: Reduce urinary calcium excretion.

#### Hypocitraturia
- **Potassium Citrate**: Increases urinary citrate (stone inhibitor) and raises urine pH.

#### Hyperuricosuria
- Potassium citrate (increases urine pH, reduces uric acid crystallization).
- **Allopurinol**: For persistent hyperuricosuria or uric acid stones.

#### Uric Acid Stones
- **Alkalize Urine (Potassium Citrate)**: Target urine pH 6.0-6.5 to dissolve/prevent uric acid stones.
- Allopurinol if serum uric acid elevated.

#### Cystine Stones
- High fluid intake.
- Alkalize urine to pH >7.
- **Tiopronin or D-Penicillamine**: Thiol agents to reduce cystine.

### Monitoring
- Repeat 24-hour urine after initiation of therapy to assess response.
- Annual metabolic evaluation for high-risk patients.
