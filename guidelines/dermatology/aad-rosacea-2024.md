## Scope
2024 AAD clinical practice guideline on the management of rosacea subtypes in adults.

## Key Recommendations

### Subtypes
- **Erythematotelangiectatic (ETR)**: Persistent central facial erythema, flushing, telangiectasias.
- **Papulopustular (PPR)**: Papules, pustules, central facial erythema.
- **Phymatous**: Skin thickening (rhinophyma).
- **Ocular**: Blepharitis, conjunctivitis, eye irritation.

### General Measures
- Gentle skin care, sun protection (SPF 30+), avoid known triggers (alcohol, spicy foods, temperature extremes).
- Barrier repair moisturizers.

### Erythema/Flushing (ETR)

#### Topical
- **Brimonidine 0.33% Gel (Mirvaso)**: Alpha-agonist vasoconstrictor; reduces erythema temporarily.
- **Oxymetazoline 1% Cream (Rhofade)**: Similar mechanism.

#### Laser/Light Therapy
- **Pulsed Dye Laser (PDL), IPL**: Effective for persistent erythema and telangiectasias.

### Papules/Pustules (PPR)

#### Topical
- **Ivermectin 1% Cream (Soolantra)**: First-line; anti-inflammatory and anti-Demodex.
- **Metronidazole 0.75-1% Cream/Gel**: Effective, well-tolerated.
- **Azelaic Acid 15% Gel**: Alternative.
- **Sodium Sulfacetamide/Sulfur**: Option.

#### Oral
- **Low-Dose Doxycycline (40 mg Modified-Release / Oracea)**: Anti-inflammatory dose; minimizes antibiotic resistance.
- **Minocycline, Tetracycline**: Alternatives.
- Systemic isotretinoin for refractory cases.

### Phymatous Rosacea
- Oral isotretinoin may help early disease.
- Surgical or laser ablation for established rhinophyma.

### Ocular Rosacea
- Lid hygiene, warm compresses.
- Topical cyclosporine or lifitegrast eye drops.
- Oral doxycycline for moderate-severe.
