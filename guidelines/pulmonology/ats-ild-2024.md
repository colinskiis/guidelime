## Scope

This guideline provides updated recommendations for the diagnosis of idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF).

## Key Recommendations

### Clinical Context
- IPF primarily affects older adults (>60)
- Risk factors: Smoking, environmental exposures, familial ILD
- Exclude identifiable causes (CTD, drug-induced, environmental)

### HRCT Patterns
**Usual Interstitial Pneumonia (UIP) Pattern:**
- Subpleural, basal predominance
- Honeycombing
- Reticular abnormalities
- Traction bronchiectasis

**Probable UIP:**
- Subpleural, basal predominance
- Reticular abnormalities with traction bronchiectasis
- No honeycombing

**Indeterminate for UIP:**
- Subpleural, basal predominance with subtle features

**Alternative Diagnosis:**
- Features suggesting other ILD

### Diagnostic Approach
**Definite UIP on HRCT:**
- Clinical-radiologic diagnosis of IPF (no biopsy needed)
- Exclude alternative causes

**Probable UIP or Indeterminate:**
- Multidisciplinary discussion (MDD)
- Consider transbronchial lung cryobiopsy (TBLC) or surgical lung biopsy
- TBLC increasingly preferred over surgical biopsy

### Bronchoscopy
- BAL: May help exclude infection, malignancy, alternative diagnoses
- TBLC: Adequate for UIP pattern confirmation in many cases
- Surgical lung biopsy: Gold standard but higher morbidity

### Genetic Testing
- Consider in familial IPF
- Telomere-related gene mutations (TERT, TERC, RTEL1)
- Surfactant mutations

### Multidisciplinary Discussion
- Pulmonologist, radiologist, pathologist input
- Essential for uncertain cases
- Improves diagnostic confidence
