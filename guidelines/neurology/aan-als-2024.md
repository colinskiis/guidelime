## Scope

This guideline provides updated recommendations for the management of patients with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS).

## Key Recommendations

### Diagnosis
- El Escorial/Awaji criteria for diagnosis
- EMG/NCS to confirm LMN involvement
- MRI to exclude structural causes
- Genetic testing (SOD1, C9orf72) for familial ALS

### Disease-Modifying Therapy
**Riluzole:**
- Prolongs survival modestly (2-3 months)
- First-line disease-modifying therapy
- Monitor LFTs

**Edaravone:**
- May slow functional decline in select patients
- IV or oral formulation
- Consider in early ALS

**Sodium Phenylbutyrate/Taurursodiol (Relyvrio):**
- May slow decline; withdrawn from market 2024
- Discuss available options with patients

**Tofersen:**
- For SOD1-mutated ALS
- Antisense oligonucleotide

### Symptom Management
**Respiratory:**
- Regular pulmonary function testing (FVC)
- Noninvasive ventilation (NIV) improves QoL and survival
- Cough assist devices
- Discuss tracheostomy if appropriate

**Nutrition:**
- Dysphagia management
- PEG placement when FVC still adequate
- Nutritional support

**Sialorrhea:**
- Glycopyrrolate, scopolamine patch
- Botulinum toxin to salivary glands
- Radiation for refractory cases

**Pseudobulbar Affect:**
- Dextromethorphan/quinidine

**Spasticity:**
- Baclofen, tizanidine
- Botulinum toxin for focal spasticity

### Multidisciplinary Care
- Multidisciplinary ALS clinic improves outcomes
- Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy
- Early palliative care involvement
- Advance care planning

### End of Life
- Hospice referral when appropriate
- Symptom management for comfort
- Ventilator withdrawal if elected
