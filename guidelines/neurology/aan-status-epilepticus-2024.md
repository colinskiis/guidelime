## Scope
2024 AAN/AES guideline update on the treatment of convulsive status epilepticus (CSE) in adults.

## Key Recommendations

### Definition
- **Status Epilepticus (SE)**: Continuous seizure activity ≥5 minutes or ≥2 seizures without return to baseline.
- Requires emergent treatment due to risk of neuronal injury, morbidity, and mortality.

### First-Line Treatment (0-5 Minutes)
- **Benzodiazepines**: Administer immediately.
  - **IV Lorazepam**: 0.1 mg/kg (max 4 mg), repeat once if needed.
  - **IM Midazolam**: 10 mg (if no IV access) – preferred in pre-hospital.
  - **IV Diazepam**: 0.15 mg/kg (max 10 mg).
  - **Rectal Diazepam, Intranasal Midazolam, Buccal Midazolam**: Alternatives.

### Second-Line Treatment (Established SE, 5-20 Minutes)
- If seizures continue after adequate benzodiazepine:
  - **Fosphenytoin**: 20 mg PE/kg IV (preferred over phenytoin).
  - **Levetiracetam**: 60 mg/kg IV (max 4500 mg).
  - **Valproate**: 40 mg/kg IV (max 3000 mg).
- All three options have similar efficacy (ESETT trial); choice based on patient factors, contraindications.

### Refractory SE (Seizures Continue ≥30 Minutes Despite Treatment)
- ICU admission, continuous EEG monitoring.
- **Anesthetic Agents**:
  - Propofol, Midazolam infusion, or Ketamine infusion.
  - Titrate to EEG burst suppression.
  - Pentobarbital for super-refractory SE.

### Supportive Care
- Airway management, IV access, glucose/thiamine if indicated, treat underlying cause.

### Investigation
- Labs (glucose, electrolytes, toxicology, AED levels), neuroimaging (CT/MRI), lumbar puncture if infection suspected.
