# ACS Best Practices Guidelines: Management of Traumatic Brain Injury

## Metadata
- **Specialty**: Trauma Surgery, Neurosurgery
- **Organization**: American College of Surgeons (ACS), Brain Trauma Foundation (BTF)
- **Year**: 2024
- **Source URL**: https://www.facs.org/for-medical-professionals/news-publications/news-and-articles/acs-brief/october-29-2024-issue/acs-releases-revised-best-practice-guidelines-in-management-of-traumatic-brain-injury/

## Scope
Comprehensive best practice guidelines for management of traumatic brain injury, including new evidence on advanced neuromonitoring, biomarkers, and tiered ICP management.

## Key Recommendations

### Initial Assessment

#### Primary Survey
| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Airway | Secure early if GCS ≤8 |
| Breathing | Avoid hypoxia (SpO2 ≥90%) |
| Circulation | Avoid hypotension (SBP ≥100 mmHg) |
| Disability | GCS, pupil examination |

#### Glasgow Coma Scale
| Score | Classification |
|-------|----------------|
| 13-15 | Mild TBI |
| 9-12 | Moderate TBI |
| 3-8 | Severe TBI |

#### Secondary Survey
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Neurological exam | Detailed assessment |
| Pupil reactivity | Size, symmetry, response |
| Motor response | Best motor response |
| Other injuries | Complete trauma survey |

### Imaging

#### CT Head Indications
| Severity | Recommendation |
|----------|----------------|
| Moderate-severe TBI | Immediate CT |
| Mild TBI with risk factors | CT indicated |
| Decision rules | NEXUS, Canadian CT Head Rule |

#### Follow-Up Imaging
| Indication | Timing |
|------------|--------|
| Neurological decline | Immediate repeat CT |
| Initial abnormality | Repeat within 6-24 hours |
| Pre-procedure | Before ICP monitor placement |

### Biomarkers (New 2024)

#### Blood-Based Biomarkers
| Biomarker | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| GFAP/UCH-L1 | May help identify need for CT in mild TBI |
| S100B | Used in some protocols |
| Clinical integration | Adjunct to clinical decision-making |

### Surgical Management

#### Indications for Surgery
| Lesion | Surgical Indication |
|--------|---------------------|
| Epidural hematoma | >30 mL, thickness >15 mm, midline shift >5 mm |
| Subdural hematoma | Thickness >10 mm, midline shift >5 mm |
| Intracerebral hemorrhage | Volume >50 mL, GCS 6-8 with decline |
| Depressed skull fracture | Open or >1 table thickness depression |

#### Decompressive Craniectomy
| Consideration | Details |
|---------------|---------|
| Refractory ICP | Consider for refractory elevation |
| Large territory | Consider for malignant edema |
| Timing | Earlier may improve outcomes |
| Patient selection | Individualized decision |

### ICP Monitoring

#### Indications
| Patient Population | Recommendation |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Severe TBI (GCS 3-8) with abnormal CT | Recommend ICP monitoring |
| Normal CT with 2+ risk factors | Consider ICP monitoring |

#### Methods
| Device | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| External ventricular drain | Gold standard; allows CSF drainage |
| Parenchymal monitor | Alternative if EVD not possible |

### Tiered ICP Management (2024 Update)

#### Target
| Parameter | Goal |
|-----------|------|
| ICP | <22 mmHg |
| CPP | 60-70 mmHg |

#### Tier 0 (Prevention)
| Intervention | Details |
|--------------|---------|
| Head elevation | 30 degrees |
| Neutral neck position | Avoid jugular compression |
| Avoid hyperthermia | Target normothermia |
| Pain/agitation control | Adequate sedation |
| Prevent constipation | Avoid Valsalva |

#### Tier 1
| Intervention | Details |
|--------------|---------|
| CSF drainage | Intermittent or continuous |
| Sedation | Propofol or midazolam |
| Osmolar therapy | Mannitol or hypertonic saline |
| CPP optimization | Vasopressors if needed |

#### Tier 2
| Intervention | Details |
|--------------|---------|
| Neuromuscular blockade | If sedation inadequate |
| Hyperventilation | Mild, temporary (PaCO2 30-35) |
| Higher osmolar therapy | Rescue dosing |
| Consider surgery | Decompressive craniectomy |

#### Tier 3
| Intervention | Details |
|--------------|---------|
| Barbiturate coma | Pentobarbital protocol |
| Decompressive craniectomy | If not already performed |
| Hypothermia | Not routinely recommended |

### Advanced Neuromonitoring (2024 Update)

#### Modalities
| Monitor | Use |
|---------|-----|
| Brain tissue oxygen (PbtO2) | Goal >20 mmHg |
| Microdialysis | Research/specialized centers |
| Continuous EEG | Seizure detection |
| Jugular venous oxygen | SjVO2 monitoring |

#### PbtO2 Monitoring
| Recommendation | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Target | PbtO2 >20 mmHg |
| Combined with ICP | May improve outcomes |
| Optimization | Adjust FiO2, CPP, transfusion |

### Medical Management

#### Blood Pressure
| Recommendation | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Avoid hypotension | SBP ≥100 mmHg (age 50-69) |
| Target SBP | ≥110 mmHg (age 15-49 or >70) |
| Vasopressors | Use if needed |

#### Oxygenation
| Recommendation | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Avoid hypoxia | SpO2 ≥90% |
| PaO2 target | >60 mmHg |
| Hyperoxia | Avoid prolonged |

#### Glucose
| Recommendation | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Avoid hypoglycemia | <80 mg/dL |
| Avoid hyperglycemia | >180 mg/dL |

#### Seizure Prophylaxis
| Recommendation | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Early seizures | Prophylaxis for 7 days |
| Agent | Levetiracetam or phenytoin |
| Late seizure prophylaxis | Not recommended |

#### DVT Prophylaxis
| Recommendation | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Mechanical | Start on admission |
| Pharmacologic | When hemorrhage stable (24-72 hours) |

### Nutrition

#### Recommendations
| Timing | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Early feeding | Within 24-72 hours |
| Caloric needs | Increased with TBI |
| Route | Enteral preferred |

### Outcome Assessment

#### Prognostication
| Consideration | Details |
|---------------|---------|
| Avoid early pessimism | May be self-fulfilling |
| Serial assessments | Over days to weeks |
| Multidisciplinary | Include rehabilitation |
