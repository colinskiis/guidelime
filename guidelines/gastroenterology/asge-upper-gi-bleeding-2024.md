## Scope
Updated 2024 ASGE guideline on the role of endoscopy in the management of acute non-variceal and variceal upper gastrointestinal (GI) bleeding.

## Key Recommendations

### Pre-Endoscopic Management
- **Resuscitation**: Hemodynamic stabilization with IV fluids and blood products (goal Hb 7-9 g/dL for most patients).
- **Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs)**: High-dose IV PPI (e.g., pantoprazole 80 mg bolus, then 8 mg/hr infusion) is reasonable before endoscopy for high-risk patients but should NOT delay EGD.
- **Risk Stratification**: Use validated scores (e.g., Glasgow-Blatchford Score) to identify patients who may be candidates for outpatient management.
- **Prokinetics**: Erythromycin (250 mg IV 30-90 min before EGD) may improve visualization by promoting gastric emptying.

### Timing of Endoscopy
- **High-Risk Patients**: EGD within 24 hours of presentation.
- **Very High-Risk/Unstable**: Consider urgent EGD after resuscitation; emergent EGD (<12 hours) for patients with hemodynamic instability or ongoing bleeding.

### Endoscopic Hemostasis (Non-Variceal)
- **High-Risk Stigmata** (active bleeding, visible vessel, adherent clot): Dual therapy recommended (e.g., injection + thermal or mechanical hemostasis). Hemoclips alone are an acceptable alternative for visible vessels.
- **Low-Risk Stigmata** (flat pigmented spot, clean base): No endoscopic therapy needed.

### Variceal Bleeding
- **Esophageal Varices**: Band ligation is the recommended endoscopic therapy.
- **Gastric Varices (GOV2, IGV1)**: Consider cyanoacrylate glue injection or TIPS.
- **Adjunctive Therapy**: Vasoactive drugs (octreotide, terlipressin) and antibiotics (ceftriaxone) should be started pre-endoscopy.

### Post-Endoscopic Management
- High-dose PPI for 72 hours post-hemostasis; then transition to oral PPI twice daily.
- H. pylori testing and eradication if positive.
