## Scope

## Key Recommendations

### Medications to Avoid in Most Older Adults
**Anticholinergics:**
- First-generation antihistamines (diphenhydramine, hydroxyzine)
- Antispasmodics (dicyclomine, hyoscyamine)
- Tricyclic antidepressants (amitriptyline, imipramine)
- Antiparkinson anticholinergics (benztropine, trihexyphenidyl)

**CNS Depressants:**
- Benzodiazepines (all)
- Nonbenzodiazepine hypnotics (zolpidem, eszopiclone)
- Meprobamate, barbiturates
- Skeletal muscle relaxants (cyclobenzaprine, methocarbamol)

**Cardiovascular:**
- Digoxin >0.125 mg/day
- Nifedipine immediate-release
- Peripheral alpha-1 blockers for hypertension (doxazosin, prazosin, terazosin)

**Gastrointestinal:**
- Metoclopramide (unless gastroparesis)
- PPIs >8 weeks without indication

**Pain Medications:**
- Meperidine
- Indomethacin
- Ketorolac
- NSAIDs chronic use (high GI/CV risk)

### Disease-Specific PIMs
- Delirium: Avoid anticholinergics, benzodiazepines, meperidine
- Dementia: Avoid anticholinergics, antipsychotics (mortality risk)
- Falls: Avoid benzodiazepines, hypnotics, antipsychotics
- Heart failure: Avoid NSAIDs, nondihydropyridine CCBs (verapamil, diltiazem)
- CKD: Avoid NSAIDs, PPIs long-term

### Drug-Drug Interactions
- Warfarin + NSAIDs/aspirin (bleeding)
- ACEi + K+-sparing diuretic (hyperkalemia)
- Opioid + benzodiazepine (respiratory depression)
- Lithium + ACEi (lithium toxicity)

### Use with Caution
- Aspirin for primary prevention >70 years
- Dabigatran if CrCl <30
- Tramadol (seizure risk, SIADH)
