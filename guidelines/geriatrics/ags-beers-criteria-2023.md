## Scope
The 2023 update to the American Geriatrics Society Beers Criteria, a list of potentially inappropriate medications (PIMs) to avoid in older adults (≥65 years).

## Key Recommendations

### Medications to Avoid (All Older Adults)
- **Benzodiazepines**: Increased risk of cognitive impairment, delirium, falls, fractures, and motor vehicle accidents.
- **Non-Benzodiazepine Hypnotics (Z-drugs)**: Zolpidem, eszopiclone, zaleplon – similar risks.
- **Strong Anticholinergics**: Antihistamines (diphenhydramine), antispasmodics (oxybutynin, tolterodine) – cognitive and fall risk.
- **Antipsychotics**: Avoid unless for schizophrenia, bipolar, or short-term antiemetic. Increased mortality in dementia.
- **First-Generation Antipsychotics**: Higher risk of EPS.
- **Sulfonylureas (Long-acting)**: Glibenclamide/glyburide – hypoglycemia risk.
- **Meperidine, Indomethacin**: High-risk opioid and NSAID.

### Medications to Avoid with Specific Conditions
- **NSAIDs**: Avoid in heart failure, CKD stage IV-V, or history of GI bleed.
- **Anticholinergics**: Avoid in dementia, delirium, constipation, BPH.
- **Central Nervous System Active Drugs**: Use caution with fall risk.

### Drug-Drug Interactions to Avoid
- Multiple CNS-active drugs together.
- Opioids + benzodiazepines.
- Warfarin + NSAIDs.
- Corticosteroids + NSAIDs.

### Medications to Use with Caution
- **Aspirin for Primary Prevention (Age >70)**: Limited benefit, increased bleeding.
- **Dabigatran**: Increased GI bleeding risk in adults ≥75 vs. other DOACs.
- **Diuretics**: Monitor for falls, electrolyte abnormalities.

### Application
- The Beers Criteria is a clinical decision-support tool, not an absolute prohibition. Always weigh benefits vs. risks for the individual patient.
