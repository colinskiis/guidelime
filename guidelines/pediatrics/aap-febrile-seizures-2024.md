## Scope

This guideline provides updated recommendations for the evaluation and management of simple febrile seizures in children aged 6 months to 60 months.

## Key Recommendations

### Definition
**Simple Febrile Seizure:**
- Generalized tonic-clonic seizure
- Duration <15 minutes
- Does not recur within 24 hours
- Child 6 months to 5 years
- Associated with fever (≥38°C/100.4°F)
- No CNS infection or metabolic cause

### Lumbar Puncture
- Not routinely recommended for well-appearing children
- Consider if:
  - Meningeal signs or symptoms
  - Complex febrile seizure
  - Not fully immunized (Hib, PCV)
  - Prior antibiotic treatment masking meningitis
  - Clinical concern for CNS infection

### EEG
- Not recommended for simple febrile seizures
- Does not predict recurrence or epilepsy

### Neuroimaging
- Not recommended for simple febrile seizures
- Not routinely needed after first simple febrile seizure

### Blood Tests
- Not routinely indicated
- Evaluate for source of fever as clinically indicated

### Treatment - Acute Seizure
- Supportive care during seizure
- Benzodiazepine for seizures lasting >5 minutes
- Treat underlying fever source

### Recurrence Prevention
**Daily Prophylactic Antiseizure Medications:**
- Not recommended (risks outweigh benefits)

**Intermittent Antipyretics:**
- Do not prevent febrile seizure recurrence
- Use for comfort only

**Intermittent Benzodiazepines:**
- Consider oral diazepam during febrile illness for recurrent febrile seizures
- Not routinely recommended after first episode

### Prognosis
- Risk of recurrence: ~30% overall
- Higher if: Age <18 months, low fever at seizure, short duration fever before seizure
- Risk of epilepsy: Slightly increased but still low (~1-2%)

### Parent Education
- Reassurance about benign nature
- Seizure first aid
- When to seek emergency care
