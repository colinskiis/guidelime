## Scope
Evidence-based guideline for evaluation and management of well-appearing, term infants 8-60 days old with fever ≥38.0°C.

## Key Recommendations by Age Group

### Infants 8-21 Days Old
- **Urinalysis and urine culture**: Obtain in all infants
- **Blood culture**: Obtain in all infants
- **CSF analysis and culture**: Obtain in all infants
- **Inflammatory markers**: Obtain (procalcitonin, ANC, CRP)
- **Disposition**: Hospitalize pending culture results
- **Antibiotics**: Parenteral antibiotics for all febrile infants in this age group

### Infants 22-28 Days Old
- **Urinalysis and urine culture**: Obtain in all infants
- **Blood culture**: Obtain in all infants
- **CSF analysis**:
  - Obtain if inflammatory markers abnormal
  - May defer if all inflammatory markers normal (shared decision-making)
- **Inflammatory markers**: Obtain (procalcitonin, ANC, CRP)
- **Disposition**:
  - Hospitalize if CSF obtained and results abnormal/pending
  - May observe at home if low-risk and reliable follow-up

### Infants 29-60 Days Old
- **Urinalysis and urine culture**: Obtain in all infants
- **Blood culture**: May obtain based on clinical judgment
- **CSF analysis**:
  - Obtain if inflammatory markers abnormal
  - May defer if all inflammatory markers normal
- **Inflammatory markers**: Obtain if UTI identified (to assess for bacteremia)
- **Disposition**: May manage as outpatient if low-risk criteria met

## Inflammatory Marker Thresholds

| Marker | Normal Value | Abnormal |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Procalcitonin | <0.5 ng/mL | ≥0.5 ng/mL |
| ANC | <4000-5000/μL | ≥4000-5000/μL |
| CRP | <20 mg/L | ≥20 mg/L |

## Low-Risk Criteria (for outpatient management)
- Well-appearing
- Normal inflammatory markers
- Negative urine (if UTI source excluded)
- No CSF pleocytosis (if LP performed)
- Reliable caregiver and follow-up within 24 hours

## Antibiotic Recommendations
- **Parenteral**: Ampicillin + gentamicin OR ampicillin + cefotaxime
- **For UTI only** (29-60 days, low-risk): Oral cephalosporin may be appropriate

## Key Concepts
- HSV evaluation: Consider in infants with risk factors or concerning findings
- Shared decision-making incorporated throughout
- Age-stratified approach reflects different risk profiles
