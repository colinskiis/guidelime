## Scope
2024 AAP/AAFP clinical practice guideline update on the diagnosis and management of acute otitis media (AOM) in children 6 months to 12 years.

## Key Recommendations

### Diagnosis
- Requires MODERATE to SEVERE bulging of the tympanic membrane (TM) OR new otorrhea not due to otitis externa.
- MILD bulging of TM PLUS recent (<48 hours) ear pain or intense TM erythema also acceptable.
- Otoscopy should demonstrate middle ear effusion.

### Severity
- **Severe AOM**: Moderate-to-severe otalgia, otalgia ≥48 hours, or temperature ≥39°C (102.2°F).
- **Non-Severe AOM**: Mild otalgia for <48 hours, temperature <39°C.

### Management

#### Age <6 Months
- Treat with antibiotics.

#### Age 6-23 Months
- **Bilateral AOM or Severe**: Treat with antibiotics.
- **Unilateral Non-Severe**: Offer antibiotic OR watchful waiting with follow-up.

#### Age ≥2 Years
- **Severe or Bilateral with Otorrhea**: Treat with antibiotics.
- **Non-Severe Unilateral or Bilateral without Otorrhea**: Watchful waiting is an option with assured follow-up.

### Antibiotic Choice
- **First-Line**: Amoxicillin 80-90 mg/kg/day divided BID (high dose to cover resistant S. pneumoniae).
- **Penicillin Allergy**: Cefdinir, cefuroxime, cefpodoxime, or azithromycin (if non-anaphylactic allergy).
- **Treatment Failure**: Amoxicillin-clavulanate or ceftriaxone IM.

### Duration
- <2 years or severe: 10 days.
- 2-5 years with mild-moderate: 7 days.
- ≥6 years with mild-moderate: 5-7 days.

### Pain Management
- Analgesics (ibuprofen, acetaminophen) for all patients.

### Prevention
- Pneumococcal and influenza vaccination.
- Avoid tobacco smoke exposure.
