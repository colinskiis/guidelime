# ESPEN Practical Guideline: Clinical Nutrition in the Intensive Care Unit

## Metadata
- **Specialty**: Critical Care, Nutrition
- **Organization**: European Society for Clinical Nutrition and Metabolism (ESPEN)
- **Year**: 2023
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.clnu.2023.07.011](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clnu.2023.07.011)
- **PMID**: [37517372](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37517372/)
- **Source URL**: https://www.clinicalnutritionjournal.com/article/S0261-5614(23)00230-3/fulltext

## Scope
Practical and partially revised guideline for clinical nutrition in critically ill adult patients, with flow charts for implementation.

## Key Recommendations

### Nutritional Assessment

#### Screening
- All ICU patients should be screened for malnutrition risk
- Use validated tools (NRS-2002, NUTRIC score)
- Consider pre-ICU nutritional status

#### Who is at High Risk?
- Pre-existing malnutrition
- Low BMI (<18.5 kg/mÂ²)
- Unintentional weight loss >10% in 6 months
- Prolonged ICU stay anticipated
- High NUTRIC score

### Timing of Nutrition

#### Enteral Nutrition (EN)
- **Initiate within 24-48 hours** of ICU admission
- Prefer early EN over delayed EN
- Prefer EN over parenteral nutrition (PN)

#### Parenteral Nutrition (PN)
- Consider early PN only if EN contraindicated/not tolerated
- In well-nourished patients, can delay PN up to 7 days if EN not feasible
- In malnourished patients, consider earlier PN supplementation

### Energy and Protein Targets

#### Energy
| Phase | Target |
|-------|--------|
| Acute phase (Days 1-3) | Start low: 70% of target or ~15-20 kcal/kg/day |
| Stabilization (Days 4-7) | Increase toward goal |
| Recovery phase (Day 7+) | Full target: 25-30 kcal/kg/day |

#### Protein
- **Target: 1.2-2.0 g/kg/day**
- Higher end for high catabolic states
- May need adjustment in renal failure
- Prioritize protein delivery over total calories

### Route of Delivery

#### Enteral Nutrition
- **Gastric feeding** is first-line
- **Post-pyloric/jejunal feeding** if:
  - High aspiration risk
  - Gastric intolerance despite prokinetics
  - Severe pancreatitis

#### Prokinetics
- Consider metoclopramide or erythromycin for gastroparesis
- Do not use routinely

### Monitoring

#### Tolerance Assessment
- Gastric residual volumes (GRV): Use with caution
- High GRV alone is not indication to stop EN
- Consider clinical signs: vomiting, abdominal distension, regurgitation

#### Glycemic Control
- Avoid hyperglycemia (target <10 mmol/L or 180 mg/dL)
- Avoid hypoglycemia

### Special Nutrients

#### Glutamine
- Not routinely recommended IV in critically ill
- Consider enteral glutamine in burns and trauma

#### Omega-3 Fatty Acids
- Not routinely recommended as pharmaconutrition
- Standard lipid emulsions acceptable

#### Antioxidants/Trace Elements
- Standard supplementation recommended
- High-dose single micronutrients not routinely recommended

### Special Populations

#### ARDS/Acute Lung Injury
- No specific EN formula recommended
- Avoid overfeeding

#### Sepsis/Septic Shock
- Start low-dose EN once hemodynamically stabilized
- Full nutrition support after acute phase

#### Acute Kidney Injury
- Protein not routinely restricted
- Adjust electrolytes as needed
- Standard or renal EN formulas

#### Obesity (BMI >30)
- Use adjusted body weight for calculations
- High protein (2-2.5 g/kg ideal body weight)
- Hypocaloric feeding may be appropriate

### Refeeding Syndrome Prevention
- In high-risk patients, start at 25-50% of target
- Monitor phosphate, potassium, magnesium daily initially
- Supplement thiamine before feeding
- Gradually increase over 3-5 days
